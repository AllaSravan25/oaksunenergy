How Solar Works

Collect

Sunlight is collected using solar panels, typically mounted on rooftops or in open areas with ample sunlight exposure. The solar panels consist of photovoltaic cells that absorb sunlight.

Convert

The absorbed sunlight is converted into electrical energy through the photovoltaic effect. Within the solar panels, photons from the sunlight excite electrons in the semiconductor material of the cells, generating a flow of direct current (DC) electricity.

Use

The generated DC electricity is then converted into alternating current (AC) electricity using an inverter. AC electricity is the standard form of electricity used in homes and businesses. The AC electricity can be used immediately to power electrical appliances, lighting, and other devices within the property. It helps reduce the reliance on grid electricity and lowers energy costs.

Store

Excess electricity generated by the solar panels can be stored for later use, especially in situations where the demand exceeds the solar production or during periods of low sunlight. Energy storage systems, such as batteries, can be employed to store the excess electricity. These batteries store the energy in the form of chemical potential, which can be converted back to electrical energy when needed.

Monitor

Monitoring systems are employed to keep track of the solar energy production, energy consumption, and battery status. Solar monitoring systems provide real-time data on the performance of the solar panels, enabling you to evaluate the system's efficiency and identify any issues that may arise. Energy monitoring systems track the usage of electricity within the property, allowing you to monitor and optimize your energy consumption patterns. Battery monitoring systems provide information on the storage capacity and status of the batteries, ensuring their efficient operation.
